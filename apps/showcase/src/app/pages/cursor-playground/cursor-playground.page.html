Cursor playground

The native cursor is fully replaced by a small dot, always white with mix-blend-mode: exclusion, so it auto-inverts correctly on any background — white on dark, black on light — with no per-target color logic. (An earlier version tinted the ring to a solid, opaque per-button color; that made labels unreadable under the fill and read worse than the plain auto-inverting white, so it was reverted.) Every interactive element (buttons, inputs) is a cursor target automatically — no attribute needed. Two distinct strategies:

  • scale (the default) — for most buttons, inputs, and links: the dot just grows to 40×40 and keeps following the pointer; the target itself is untouched.
  • fill (outline/ghost buttons only — solid variants like destructive already read clearly on their own, so they just scale) — the target's own border/background hide, and the cursor morphs into a pill exactly overlaying it (adopting its actual border-radius). Because the ring blends rather than covers, the label stays fully readable throughout.
